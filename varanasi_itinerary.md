# Varanasi (Kashi) Asthi Visarjan & Temple Yatra Plan

This plan is customized for your journey with Hari Kumar Andem to perform the sacred rites of immersing your mother's ashes (*Asthi Visarjan*) in the Ganga and visiting the key temples of Kashi. It incorporates the timing constraints you provided and integrates traditional Kashi customs and local guides.

## 📿 Detailed Day-by-Day Itinerary

### Day 1: July 6 (Arrival & Preparation)
*   **08:00 PM – 09:30 PM:** Arrive at Varanasi Airport, hire a pre-booked cab (highly recommended over street taxis to avoid delays), and check into your hotel. 
    > [!TIP]
    > **Hotel Location:** Since most key temples and ghats are in the old city, staying near **Godowlia Crossing**, **Dashashwamedh Ghat**, or **Luxa Road** will save you hours of travel time in narrow lanes where vehicles cannot go.
*   **09:30 PM onwards:** Have a light dinner and sleep early to prepare for the 5:00 AM start.

---

### Day 2: July 7 (Morning Slot: 5:00 AM – 08:15 AM) — Kaal Bhairav & Core Temples
This morning slot is structured to visit Kaal Bhairav first to seek his permission, followed by the main temple cluster in the narrow alleys.

*   **05:00 AM – 05:40 AM: Kaal Bhairav Temple (Kotwal of Kashi)**
    *   *Significance:* Prioritized as the very first temple of your pilgrimage. Traditionally, pilgrims visit Kaal Bhairav to seek permission before visiting other shrines. Tie a black thread (*Kashi Dhaaga*) around your wrist for protection.
*   **05:40 AM – 06:10 AM: Travel to Lalita Ghat**
    *   Take an e-rickshaw to the central ghats area to enter the pedestrian alleys.
*   **06:10 AM – 06:40 AM: Ugra Varahi Temple**
    *   *Significance:* This underground temple dedicated to Goddess Varahi (a protecting deity) is unique because it is open **only during the early morning hours** (typically 5:30 AM to 7:30 AM). Visiting it in this slot is a must.
*   **06:40 AM – 07:40 AM: Kashi Vishwanath Temple (Lord Shiva)**
    *   Perform your darshan of the primary Shiva Lingam (Sugam Darshan VIP tickets recommended). Observe the giant stone Nandi in the outer courtyard facing the Gyanvapi Well, which marks the location of the original Swayambhu Lingam.
*   **07:40 AM – 08:15 AM: Maa Annapurna, Visalakshi & Sakshi Ganapathi**
    *   **Maa Annapurna:** Worship the Goddess of sustenance and see the sacred Annapurna Yantra in the side room.
    *   **Visalakshi Temple:** One of the 51 Shakti Peethas (pray to the original ancient stone idol).
    *   **Sakshi Ganapathi:** A small temple where you "register" your pilgrimage with Ganesha, who acts as the witness (*Sakshi*) to your Kashi Yatra.

---

### Day 2: July 7 (Mid-Day Slot: 8:00 AM – 12:00 PM) — Asthi Visarjan & Sacred Noon Bath
This block is dedicated entirely to your mother's final rites and the cleansing baths.

*   **08:00 AM – 08:30 AM:** Reach Kedar Ghat or Hanuman Ghat carrying your mother's ashes.
*   **08:30 AM – 10:15 AM: Asthi Visarjan Ceremony**
    *   Engage a local Telugu-speaking priest (*Pujari*) beforehand. 
    *   **The Ritual:** You and Hari Kumar Andem will perform the *Pinda Pradhanam* (offerings) on the clean steps of Kedar/Hanuman Ghat (which is much cleaner and less crowded than Manikarnika). Then, hire a wooden boat to take you to the middle of the Ganga. Under the priest's direction, perform the Vedic prayers (Sankalpa with your mother's name and Gotra) and gently immerse her ashes (*Asthikalu*) into the river.
*   **10:15 AM – 11:00 AM: Holy Dip in Ganga**
    *   After immersing the ashes, take your holy dip (*Snanam*) in the Ganges at Kedar Ghat, which is safer for family bathing.
*   **11:00 AM – 11:45 AM: Manikarnika Kund Snanam**
    *   Optional but highly recommended: Head to the historic *Chakra Pushkarini Kund* beside Manikarnika Ghat. Traditional customs emphasize performing a bath or sprinkling water (*Prokshana*) near the **12 Noon** hour, as Lord Shiva, Goddess Parvati, and all the Devas are believed to gather there at midday.
*   **11:45 AM – 12:00 PM:** Change into dry clothes and pay your respects/donations to the priest.

---

### Day 2: July 7 (Rest Slot: 12:00 PM – 4:00 PM) — Lunch & Rest
*   **12:00 PM – 01:30 PM:** Have a pure vegetarian, *Sattvik* lunch (without onion or garlic, out of respect for the ancestors on the day of rituals).
*   **01:30 PM – 04:00 PM:** Rest at your hotel to recover from the morning activities.

---

### Day 2: July 7 (Evening Slot: 4:00 PM – 09:30 PM) — Southern Temples & Ganga Aarti
This slot covers the southern temples of the city, ending with the famous evening Ganga Aarti at the center.

*   **04:00 PM – 05:00 PM: Sankat Mochan Hanuman Temple**
    *   *Significance:* Founded by Goswami Tulsidas. It features high spiritual energy and is much more peaceful to visit in the late afternoon to avoid the massive night crowds. (Mobile phones and bags must be deposited outside).
*   **05:00 PM – 05:30 PM: Durga Temple (Durga Kund) & Tridev Temple**
    *   Located right near Sankat Mochan. The red Durga Kund temple is very historic.
*   **05:50 PM – 07:45 PM: Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat**
    *   Board a pre-booked wooden boat from the ghat steps by 5:50 PM to secure a good spot. Watching the synchronized, lamp-lit ritual from the water at sunset (starting around 6:45 PM) offers a majestic, unobstructed view.
*   **08:00 PM – 09:30 PM: Dinner & Varanasi Street Food**
    *   Enjoy local specialties like *Tamatar Chaat* and *Lassi* at famous local spots.
*   **09:30 PM:** Return to the hotel.

---

### Day 3: July 8 (Morning Slot: 3:00 AM – 8:00 AM) — Final Darshan, Assi Ghat & Departure
A highly spiritual and active final morning in Varanasi before heading back to Bangalore.

*   **03:00 AM – 05:00 AM: Kashi Vishwanath Sugam Darshan / Mangala Aarti**
    *   *VIP/Sugam Darshan:* Book a fast-track entry ticket online in advance to beat the massive day queues. The early morning environment is quiet and sacred.
    *   *Mangala Aarti Option:* Securing Mangala Aarti tickets (3:00 AM – 4:00 AM) allows you to witness the first ritual of the day. *Sparsha Darshan* (touching the Jyotirlinga) is sometimes allowed immediately after the Aarti, depending on the day's temple crowd management rules.
    *   *Note:* Bhasma Aarti is exclusive to Mahakaleshwar Temple in Ujjain; in Kashi Vishwanath, the early morning ritual is the *Mangala Aarti*.
*   **05:15 AM – 06:15 AM: Subah-e-Banaras at Assi Ghat**
    *   *Experience:* Directly travel south to Assi Ghat to witness the morning Ganga Aarti at dawn, followed by Vedic chanting, a yoga session, and a peaceful Hindustani classical music recital on the steps.
*   **06:15 AM – 07:15 AM: New Vishwanath Temple (VT) at BHU**
    *   Located inside the green, quiet Banaras Hindu University campus. This massive marble structure is spacious, clean, and highly peaceful in the early morning.
*   **07:15 AM – 08:00 AM:** Return to your hotel, pack your bags, check out, and have breakfast.
*   **08:00 AM – 09:15 AM:** Take a cab from the hotel to Varanasi Airport (Babatpur).
*   **10:00 AM:** Board your return flight to Bangalore.

## 💡 Important Tips & Guidelines

1.  **Mobile Phones & Belongings:**
    *   Mobile phones, cameras, smartwatches, leather belts, and wallets are **strictly prohibited** inside Kashi Vishwanath Temple and Sankat Mochan. Lockers are available outside, but it is much safer and faster to leave these items in your hotel room.
2.  **Priest Arrangement:**
    *   To prevent harassment or overcharging at the ghats, arrange a priest (purohit) in advance. You can ask your hotel receptionist to recommend a trusted Telugu-speaking priest or book via a registered ashram (e.g., Andhra Ashram).
3.  **Dress Code:**
    *   Wear traditional Indian attire (dhoti/kurta-pyjama for men) for the rituals. Avoid shorts, t-shirts, or western wear during the religious ceremonies.
4.  **Local Commute:**
    *   Traffic inside Varanasi is heavy and narrow lanes are blocked to cars. Use **e-rickshaws** for road travel and **boats** for river travel to avoid traffic jams.
