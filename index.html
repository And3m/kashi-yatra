The Pilgrimage

In Loving Memory

Varanasi (Kashi) is the city of Lord Shiva, a place of spiritual liberation where the boundary between the material and the divine dissolves. On the 7th of July, both brothers embark on this sacred journey along the banks of the holy Ganges to perform the Asthi Visarjan (immersion of ashes) for their beloved Mother, ensuring her soul finds eternal peace and liberation (Moksha).

Vijay Andem and his elder brother, **Hari Kumar Andem**, are traveling together from Bangalore to Varanasi to perform these solemn rites, while also visiting the legendary temples of Kashi. Following the ancient traditions and local heritage customs, this itinerary balances their sacred duties with an immersive temple pilgrimage, uncovering Kashi’s hidden spiritual secrets along the way.

"Kashi is the light of liberation. Dying here, or immersing the remains of the departed in the Ganges, leads the soul directly to the feet of Lord Shiva." — Kashi Khanda, Skanda Purana

08:00 PM (July 6)

Arrival in Varanasi & Hotel Check-in

Arrival at Varanasi Airport and transfer to the hotel to rest before a very early morning start.

Read Details

Logistics: Your flight departs Bangalore at 4:00 PM and arrives in Varanasi (Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport, Babatpur) at approximately 8:00 PM. The airport is about 24–26 km from the central ghats/temple area. Travel time by pre-booked cab is roughly 1 to 1.5 hours depending on city traffic.

Accommodations: Check into your hotel. It is highly recommended to stay near Godowlia Crossing or Luxa Road, which allows easy walking/e-rickshaw access to the Ghats and the Kashi Vishwanath temple.

Goal for the night: Keep dinner light, organize your clothing and documentation for the next morning, and sleep early. Your temple tour starts at 4:00 AM sharp.

05:00 AM – 08:15 AM (July 7)

Morning Temple Tour: Kaal Bhairav & The Sacred Cluster

Start the journey by seeking permission from Kaal Bhairav, followed by Ugra Varahi, Kashi Vishwanath, Annapurna, and Visalakshi.

Read Details

This morning block is carefully structured to prioritize the Kotwal of Kashi first, followed by the central temple cluster:

  • Kaal Bhairav Temple (05:00 AM - 05:40 AM): Prioritized as the very first temple of your pilgrimage. As the spiritual guardian of Kashi, it is customary to visit him first to seek permission for your stay and yatra. Tie the black protective Kashi thread (*Kashi Dhaaga*) around your wrist.
  • Ugra Varahi Temple (06:10 AM - 06:40 AM): Travel by e-rickshaw to Lalita Ghat. Varahi Devi is open only early in the morning (5:30 AM - 7:30 AM) and is highly powerful.
  • Kashi Vishwanath Temple (06:40 AM - 07:40 AM): Perform your darshan of the primary Shiva Lingam. (Sugam Darshan VIP tickets recommended). Observe the stone Nandi facing the Gyanvapi Well, indicating the location of the original Swayambhu Lingam.
  • Maa Annapurna, Visalakshi & Sakshi Ganapathi (07:40 AM - 08:15 AM): Worship the Goddess of nourishment (Annapurna), the Shakti Peetha (Visalakshi), and register your pilgrimage with Sakshi Ganapathi. All are located in the immediate lanes next to Kashi Vishwanath.
08:00 AM – 12:00 PM (July 7)

Asthi Visarjan & Sacred Noon Bath (Kedar Ghat)

Performing the sacred ash immersion for Mother at Kedar Ghat (Telugu Hub), followed by the highly auspicious midday bath.

Read Details

The core spiritual obligation of your journey. Ensure this is completed before 12:00 PM:

  1. The Ritual (08:30 AM - 10:15 AM): Meet your pre-arranged Telugu-speaking priest (*Pujari*) at **Kedar Ghat** or **Hanuman Ghat** (which is much cleaner and less commercialized than Manikarnika Ghat). Perform the *Pinda Pradhanam* (prayers and offerings) on the ghat steps, then hire a private wooden boat to row to the center of the holy Ganges. Under the priest's direction, perform the Vedic prayers (Sankalpa with your mother's name and Gotra) and gently immerse her ashes (*Asthikalu*) into the river.
  2. Holy Dip (10:15 AM - 11:00 AM): Take your cleansing dip (*Snanam*) in the Ganges at Kedar Ghat, which is safer and cleaner for family bathing.
  3. Manikarnika Kund Snanam (11:00 AM - 11:45 AM): Optional but highly recommended: Head to the historic *Chakra Pushkarini Kund* near Manikarnika Ghat. Traditional customs emphasize performing a bath (*Madhyannika Snanam*) near the **12 Noon** hour, as Lord Shiva, Goddess Parvati, and celestial beings are believed to gather there at midday. You can sprinkle this holy water over your head (*Prokshana*) for blessings.
  4. Completion: Change into fresh dry clothes and pay your respects/donations to the priest.

Rest, Reflection & Light Lunch

Returning to the hotel to rest, dry off, and have a simple, pure vegetarian Sattvik lunch.

Sattvik Meal: After performing ancestral rites, it is custom to eat light, pure vegetarian food (Sattvik - prepared without onion and garlic). Good local options include simple thalis at heritage hotels or local traditional outlets near your lodging.

04:00 PM – 09:30 PM (July 7)

Evening Tour: Southern Temples & Ganga Aarti

Visiting the powerful Sankat Mochan and Durga temples, followed by watching the Ganga Aarti from a boat.

Read Details

This block covers the southern sector of the city and ends with the majestic sunset Ganga Aarti at the center:

  • Sankat Mochan Hanuman Temple (04:00 PM - 05:00 PM): Located in the southern Lanka sector. Founded by Goswami Tulsidas, it is highly spiritual. It is much easier to visit in the late afternoon to avoid the heavy night crowds. Keep all mobile phones and bags locked in your room or cab.
  • Durga Temple & Tridev Temple (05:00 PM - 05:30 PM): Located right near Sankat Mochan. The historic red Durga Kund temple is dedicated to Goddess Durga and features a large holy pond.
  • Dashashwamedh Ganga Aarti (05:50 PM - 07:45 PM): Head back to the center of the city. Board a pre-booked wooden boat from the ghat steps by 5:50 PM. Enjoying the sunset and watching the synchronized, lamp-lit ritual from the water (starting around 6:45 PM) offers a peaceful, majestic view.
  • Post-Aarti Dinner (08:00 PM - 09:30 PM): Enjoy Varanasi's famous street foods (Tamatar Chaat, Lassi) before returning to your hotel.

Morning Serenity: Subah-e-Banaras

Enjoying morning Aarti and music at Assi Ghat, followed by a visit to the peaceful BHU New Vishwanath Temple.

Spiritual Wisdom

Sacred Secrets & Traditional Wisdom of Kashi

Significant details and lesser-known traditions to enrich your spiritual pilgrimage.

Ugra Varahi underground

Goddess Varahi is Kashi's fierce guardian. Her temple is underground and lacks windows. It is open for only two hours daily (5:30 AM – 7:30 AM). Visiting requires precise timing but grants immense spiritual protection.

Manikarnika Kund Noon Dip

The Chakra Pushkarini Kund predates the Ganges itself, dug by Lord Vishnu. Traditional Kashi customs emphasize the *Madhyannika Snanam* (noon bath) here, as Lord Shiva and all celestial beings are believed to bathe here at 12:00 PM daily.

The Gyanvapi Nandi

Inside the Kashi Vishwanath corridor, locate the massive stone Nandi. Unlike typical temples where Nandi faces the active altar, this Nandi faces the Gyanvapi Well, pointing to where the original Swayambhu Lingam was hidden.

Sakshi Ganapathi

A small temple en route. Legend states that Lord Ganesha acts as the witness (*Sakshi*) to your pilgrimage. Pilgrims stop here to tell Ganapathi their name and gotra to "register" their visit to Kashi.

Kaal Bhairav Permission

Kaal Bhairav is the Kotwal (police chief) of Kashi. It is traditional to visit him to seek "permission" to enter and safely leave Kashi. A black protective thread (*Kashi Dhaaga*) is tied around your wrist here.

Maa Visalakshi Shakti Peeth

This is one of the 51 Shakti Peethas representing the ears/earrings of Sati. The temple contains two idols of the Goddess: the swayambhu (self-manifested) stone behind, and the worshiped idol in front.

Travel Tips & Local Guides

Temple Mobile Restriction

Phones, cameras, smartwatches, leather belts, and wallets are strictly banned inside Kashi Vishwanath and Sankat Mochan. Leave them in your hotel safe to avoid long deposit locker queues.

Local Commuting

Cars cannot navigate the narrow alleys of Varanasi and are blocked from Godowlia. Rely on e-rickshaws for roads, and use hand-rowed or motor boats to travel along the ghats.

Varanasi Street Food

Varanasi is famous for its food. Don't miss Tamatar Chaat at Deena Chat Bhandar, Kachori Sabzi at Kachori Gali in the morning, and the thick, hand-churned saffron Lassi at Blue Lassi.

Telugu Pilgrim Support & Mutts

Reach out to **Sri Rama Taraka Andhra Ashram** or **Sri Kumaraswamy Mutt** near Kedar Ghat. They provide authentic Telugu food, clean lodging, and connect you with trusted, verified Telugu-speaking priests.

Beware of Local Scams

Ignore anyone near Manikarnika requesting "wood donations" for the poor (this is a major scam). Settle boat prices clearly beforehand as a fixed package (for the entire boat, round trip) to avoid extra demands mid-river.
